# Scope File: Division 07 — Thermal & Moisture Protection

**Load when:** Query contains references to waterproofing, dampproofing, insulation, air barrier, vapor retarder, roofing, membrane, flashing, coping, fireproofing, SFRM, intumescent, sealant, caulk, expansion joint, firestopping, or any specification section 07 XX 00.

**Red flags and coordination matrix checks are in the always-loaded CLAUDE.md. This file provides the detailed cross-reference lists and absence checklists for targeted queries.**

---

## Sub-Scope 07A: Waterproofing & Dampproofing

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves below-grade waterproofing, foundation waterproofing, plaza deck waterproofing, or wet-area waterproofing, pull ALL of the following before responding:

- Architectural foundation plans and sections — waterproofing extent and termination points.
- Structural foundation plans — concrete wall and footing configurations affecting membrane application.
- Civil/site plans — site drainage, water table elevation, hydrostatic conditions.
- Geotechnical report — groundwater level, dewatering, soil drainage.
- Spec 07 10 00 — Dampproofing and Waterproofing.
- Spec 07 11 00 — Dampproofing.
- Spec 07 13 00 — Sheet Waterproofing.
- Spec 07 14 00 — Fluid-Applied Waterproofing.
- Spec 07 16 00 — Cementitious and Reactive Waterproofing.
- Spec 07 18 00 — Traffic Coatings (vehicular/pedestrian).
- Spec 07 15 00 — Sheet Metal Flashing and Trim (termination details).
- Spec 07 92 00 — Joint Sealants (at terminations and transitions).
- Division 31 — Excavation and backfill affecting waterproofing protection.
- **RFI log** — check for any RFIs addressing waterproofing conditions.
- **Submittal register** — check for approved waterproofing product submittal and any reviewer annotations.

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Waterproofing membrane type and manufacturer specified.
- [ ] Protection board or drainage mat over membrane specified.
- [ ] Waterstop at construction joints in below-grade concrete.
- [ ] Membrane termination details at grade transition, substrate changes, and penetrations.
- [ ] Foundation drainage system (perimeter drain, drainage board) shown on civil or architectural.
- [ ] Pre-application substrate preparation requirements (surface profile, moisture content, priming).
- [ ] Warranty duration and coverage (standard vs. extended, leak-free vs. material-only).
- [ ] Flood testing or leak testing requirements for horizontal waterproofing (plaza decks, green roofs, planters).
- [ ] Submittal approved for waterproofing product (check register).
- [ ] Any RFIs addressing waterproofing conditions or termination details (check log).

### Sequencing Context

Waterproofing is on the critical path for backfill. The sequence:
1. Concrete walls cured and surfaces prepared.
2. Waterproofing membrane applied and inspected.
3. Protection board and drainage mat installed.
4. Foundation drain installed (Division 33).
5. Backfill placed with material and compaction methods that will not damage membrane.

**POINT OF NO RETURN:** Once backfill is placed, the waterproofing is inaccessible. Any penetrations through waterproofed assemblies (utility sleeves, wall ties) must be sealed before burial.

## Sub-Scope 07B: Insulation & Air/Vapor Barriers

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves thermal insulation, air barriers, vapor retarders, or envelope thermal performance:

- Architectural wall sections — insulation location (cavity, continuous exterior, interior).
- Architectural roof sections — insulation type and thickness.
- Energy code compliance documentation (if included).
- Spec 07 21 00 — Thermal Insulation.
- Spec 07 22 00 — Roof and Deck Insulation.
- Spec 07 26 00 — Vapor Retarders.
- Spec 07 27 00 — Air Barriers (fluid-applied, sheet, or self-adhered).
- Division 01 or project-specific energy compliance sections.
- Architectural details at EVERY transition: wall-to-roof, wall-to-foundation, wall-to-window, wall-to-curtain-wall — for continuity of thermal and air barrier planes.
- **Submittal register** — check for approved insulation and air barrier product submittals.

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Insulation R-value or thickness specified per assembly (walls, roof, slab edge, foundation).
- [ ] Air barrier system specified with product, application method, substrate preparation.
- [ ] Vapor retarder location and permeance rating specified (verify correct side of insulation for climate zone).
- [ ] Continuity details at EVERY transition and penetration.
- [ ] Compatibility of air barrier with adjacent materials (adhesion to CMU, concrete, sheathing, steel).
- [ ] Thermal bridging addressed at shelf angles, window supports, cladding brackets.
- [ ] Approved submittal for air barrier system (check register).

### Sequencing Context

Air barrier is applied to exterior sheathing BEFORE cladding. The sequence:
1. Structural backup and sheathing complete.
2. Air barrier applied.
3. Window rough openings prepared with sill pan flashing integrated into air barrier.
4. Windows/doors installed and sealed to air barrier.
5. Cladding installed over air barrier.

**POINT OF NO RETURN:** Once cladding is installed, the air barrier is inaccessible. Every transition, penetration, and window interface must be verified before cladding closes it.

## Sub-Scope 07C: Roofing

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves roofing systems, roof details, roof drainage, or roof-mounted equipment:

- Architectural roof plan — slopes, drainage pattern, penetrations, access, equipment locations.
- Architectural roof details — membrane termination at parapet, edge, expansion joints, curbs, drains, scuppers, penetrations.
- Structural roof framing — slope (structural vs. tapered insulation), deck type, opening reinforcement.
- Mechanical roof plan — RTU locations, duct penetrations, pipe penetrations, equipment supports.
- Electrical roof plan — conduit penetrations, rooftop equipment.
- Plumbing plans — roof drain locations, overflow drains, vent pipe penetrations.
- Spec 07 50 00 — Membrane Roofing.
- Spec 07 52 00 — Modified Bitumen.
- Spec 07 54 00 — Thermoplastic (TPO, PVC).
- Spec 07 55 00 — EPDM.
- Spec 07 60 00 — Flashing and Sheet Metal.
- Spec 07 62 00 — Sheet Metal Flashing and Trim.
- Spec 07 70 00 — Roof Specialties (copings, gravel stops, fascia).
- Spec 07 72 00 — Roof Accessories (hatches, vents, walkway pads, snow guards).
- Spec 07 84 00 — Firestopping (at roof penetrations through rated assemblies).
- Division 22 — Roof drain bodies, overflow drains, conductor pipe locations.
- **RFI log** — check for any RFIs about roof conditions, penetrations, or equipment.
- **Submittal register** — check for approved roofing system submittal and warranty documentation.

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Roof membrane system type and manufacturer specified.
- [ ] Roof slope/drainage plan with minimum slope indicated (1/4" per foot minimum).
- [ ] Tapered insulation layout or structural slope noted.
- [ ] Roof drain and overflow drain locations shown with sizing.
- [ ] Parapet cap/coping details with flashing termination.
- [ ] Roof edge detail at all perimeter conditions (parapet, edge, area divider).
- [ ] Curb details for all roof-mounted equipment with minimum curb height.
- [ ] Pipe penetration flashing detail (pitch pocket vs. boot vs. custom flashing).
- [ ] Roof access path (walkway pads or pavers) from hatch to equipment.
- [ ] Warranty requirements (system vs. manufacturer NDL, wind speed rating).
- [ ] FM Global rating if required by insurance.
- [ ] Phasing/tie-in details if re-roofing adjacent to existing.
- [ ] Roofing submittal approved with warranty pre-approval documentation (check register).
- [ ] Any RFIs about roof conditions (check log).

### Sequencing Context

See Section 3.2 of CLAUDE.md (Roofing Sequence) for the full sequencing chain.

Critical note: Roof drain bodies must be set by the plumber BEFORE the roofer can tie in membrane. This is a two-trade coordination point. If the plumber is not scheduled ahead of the roofer, the roof cannot be completed.

## Sub-Scope 07D: Fireproofing

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves structural fireproofing, SFRM, or intumescent coatings:

- Structural plans — member sizes (thickness varies by W/D ratio).
- Life safety plans — fire-rating requirements by area.
- Spec 07 81 00 — Applied Fireproofing.
- Spec 07 82 00 — Board Fireproofing.
- UL assembly designations on structural or life safety drawings.
- Division 05 — structural steel (fireproofing applied after erection, before MEP attachment).

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Fireproofing type specified (SFRM vs. intumescent vs. board).
- [ ] UL assembly designations referenced with required hourly ratings.
- [ ] Fireproofing thickness schedule by member size (or reference to calculated values).
- [ ] Patching requirements for MEP attachment points and damage.
- [ ] Adhesion/cohesion testing requirements (bond pull test values).
- [ ] Approved submittal for fireproofing product (check register).

### Sequencing Context

Fireproofing creates a critical sequencing constraint: ALL MEP hangers and supports should be attached to structure BEFORE fireproofing is applied. After fireproofing, any MEP attachment requires cutting through SFRM, attaching the hanger, and patching — which requires a patching protocol, a listed patch material, and inspection.

If the MEP trades are not ahead of fireproofing, every hanger becomes a coordination event.

## Sub-Scope 07E: Joint Sealants

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves caulking, sealants, expansion joint sealants, or weatherproofing joints:

- Architectural exterior elevations and details — sealant joint locations at cladding, windows, doors, expansion joints.
- Architectural interior details — sealant at wet areas, countertop-to-wall, fixture-to-wall.
- Spec 07 92 00 — Joint Sealants.
- Spec 07 95 00 — Expansion Joint Assemblies.
- Division 04 — masonry control and expansion joints.
- Division 08 — perimeter sealant at window and door frames.
- Division 09 — sealant at interior transitions.

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Sealant types specified by location (silicone, polyurethane, hybrid — with substrate compatibility matrix).
- [ ] Joint width-to-depth ratio specified (2:1 minimum with backer rod).
- [ ] Primer requirements identified by substrate type.
- [ ] Interior sealant specified as mildew-resistant for wet areas.
- [ ] Movement joint sealant joint design (width calculated for thermal movement range).
- [ ] Approved submittal for each sealant type (check register).

## Sub-Scope 07F: Firestopping

### Cross-Reference Triggers

When a query involves firestopping at penetrations through rated assemblies:

- Life safety plans — rated wall and floor locations.
- Architectural partition type schedule — rated partition types.
- MEP plans — all penetrations through rated assemblies.
- Spec 07 84 00 — Firestopping.
- UL system designations for firestop assemblies.
- **Scope assignment** — verify which trade is responsible for firestopping (this is the most commonly unresolved scope question in construction).

### Absence Detection Checklist

- [ ] Firestopping specification section present in project manual.
- [ ] Installing trade explicitly named (often it is not — flag as scope gap per CLAUDE.md Section 7.3).
- [ ] UL system designations referenced for each penetration type (pipe, conduit, cable tray, duct, mixed).
- [ ] Through-penetration vs. membrane-penetration requirements distinguished.
- [ ] Inspection and documentation requirements (per-penetration photo documentation is becoming standard).
- [ ] Approved submittal for firestop systems by penetration type (check register).

### Sequencing Context

Firestopping happens AFTER all MEP trades have completed their penetrations through rated assemblies but BEFORE the second side of drywall is closed (for wall penetrations) or ceiling is closed (for floor penetrations). This creates a scheduling dependency:

1. First-side drywall complete.
2. MEP rough-in complete through rated walls/floors.
3. Firestopping installed and inspected.
4. Second-side drywall closed.

If firestopping is missed before closure, it requires destructive investigation to verify and install — one of the most expensive re-work scenarios in commercial construction.

## Division 07 Coordination Overlaps

These are detailed here; the summary is in CLAUDE.md Coordination Matrix items #4, #6, #7, #8, #9, #10, #19.

**With Structural (Division 05):** Fireproofing on structural steel must be in place before MEP attachment or a patching protocol is required. Verify patching is in spec and assigned.

**With Masonry (Division 04):** Through-wall flashing must be integrated with masonry coursing. Shelf angle soft joints require compatible sealant. Weep hole spacing coordinates with flashing.

**With Openings (Division 08):** Perimeter sealant at all window/door frames. Flashing at window/door heads and sills. Air barrier integration at window frames (the #1 air leakage location in buildings — CLAUDE.md Red Flag 4.2).

**With All MEP (Divisions 21–28):** Every penetration through a waterproofed, air-barrier-protected, or fire-rated assembly requires proper treatment (flashing, sealing, or firestopping). This is the single largest category of coordination failure in construction.

**With Cladding (Division 07/08):** Air barrier and thermal insulation continuity through cladding attachment zones. Thermal bridging at shelf angles, window supports, and cladding brackets.

**With Roofing (Division 07C):** Roof penetrations by all MEP trades require curbs, flashings, and walkway access. Plumber must set drain bodies before roofer can flash.
